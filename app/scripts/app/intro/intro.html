The Medical Informatics Platform

Medicine is experiencing a data explosion. The Medical Informatics Platform (MIP) aims to federate and analyse the huge volumes of data on neurological and psychiatric diseases. This will allow researchers to identify the biological changes associated with diseases and open possibilities for early diagnosis and personalised medicine.

The MIP currently has three goals:

  1. Build the tools to federate clinical data which is currently locked in hospital and research archives;
  2. Recruit hospitals to contribute to and use the platform;
  3. Develop tools which make it possible to extract biological signatures of diseases from multi-level data.

The MIP provides methods to analyse federated data from hospitals, research centres and biobanks. Clinical scientist can develop, share and release their research. The MIP aims to bring together people across disciplines who can contribute to the design and development of the services which the MIP provides.

You can use the MIP

  • as a clinician, for objective diagnoses and treatment of brain disease;
  • as a neuroscientist, for the application and testing of new models and methods;
  • as a pharmaceutical or biotech company, for disease target discovery.
